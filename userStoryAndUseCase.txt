USER STORY: THE DATA-DRIVEN REPRESENTATIVE
-------------------------------------------
As a: Ward City Councilor
I want to: Instantly visualize the intersection of housing affordability and transit usage across my specific constituency compared to the city-wide average.
So that: I can advocate for targeted infrastructure investments and social programs that address the unique socio-economic pressures of my residents during budget sessions.

Acceptance Criteria:
- SYMBOLIC IDENTIFICATION: I can quickly find my ward's neighbourhoods by their 2-letter atomic symbols (e.g., 'Me' for Métis).
- OUTLIER DETECTION: I can identify "islands" in the grid—neighbourhoods that are highly affordable but car-centric (Top-Left) vs. sustainable but priced out (Bottom-Right).

USE CASE: UC-DEM-001 - IDENTIFYING TRANSIT DESERTS IN AFFORDABLE ZONES
---------------------------------------------------------------------
PRIMARY ACTOR: Senior Urban Planner / Public Service Lead
STAKEHOLDERS: City Councilor, Transit Authority, Housing Department

1. DESCRIPTION:
A public service employee is tasked with identifying where a new "Express Bus" route should be prioritized. They need to find areas where population density is high, incomes are modest, and current sustainable transit usage is low.

2. PRECONDITIONS:
- The municipality has uploaded its latest census and real estate CSV data.
- The Periodic Grid is set to default normalization (Affordability on Y-axis, Sustainability on X-axis).

3. MAIN SUCCESS SCENARIO:
- SURVEYING THE LANDSCAPE: The Planner opens the Grid View and scans the "Blue" zone (left side). These are neighbourhoods with low Sustainable Mode % (car-centric).
- FILTERING FOR NEED: The Planner observes the top-left quadrant—neighbourhoods like Athlone (An). These areas are affordable (Top) but lack transit usage (Left).
- DEEP-DIVE ANALYSIS: The Planner clicks on a cell. The Analysis Panel reveals high population counts, suggesting a high ROI for transit investment.
- AI INSIGHT GENERATION: The Planner triggers Gemini AI. The AI highlights that while the area is an affordable entry point, its socio-economic standing is hampered by transit isolation.
- REPORTING: The Planner takes a screenshot of the "Atomic Clustering" to show the Infrastructure Committee exactly where the equity gap exists.

4. POST-CONDITION:
The planning department has a visual "Heatmap" of equity gaps, allowing them to justify a multi-million dollar transit expansion project using accessible, evidence-based visualization.
